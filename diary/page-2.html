entry 2 - february 12







mrs. danvers brought me tea in the rose garden this afternoon. she’s always so formal, so rigid — but her eyes? sharp as a hawk’s, watching every move i make like a loyal sentinel with a secret grudge. i asked her about the household accounts, pretending to care about numbersreally, i wanted to remind her who's really in charge here. she hates that, but loves the game. she craves control even if it’s just over the petty ledgers..

the garden was heavy with the scent of roses and something darker — wet earth, decay lurking just beneath the surface. perfect for secrets. i felt my nerves twitching under the summer sun. it’s strange how peaceful places always make the worst conspiracies feel close.

mrs. danvers lowered her voice, as if afraid the petals themselves might overhear. she said, “the servants whisper, miss. there’s talk in the kitchens. about your place here.” i smiled thinlylet them whisper. let the gossip swirl. it’s all smoke, and smoke blinds. but inside i was already planning my next move. the castle’s halls aren’t as safe as they think.

later, i wandered through the empty corridors, my footsteps echoing like a pulse in the quiet. i passed maxim’s study and saw his jacket thrown carelessly on a chair. i traced a finger over the fabriche’s wearing his secrets like this coat—heavy and close, but ragged at the edges..

the afternoon faded, the shadows stretching longer, creeping closer. i know the house is watching me as much as i’m watching it. and soon, everything will change.

sometimes i think this house is alive. and it hates the truth more than anyone.
